About Me

I am a master's student at School of Software & Microelectronics at Peking University, where I also work as a research assistant in the Center on Frontiers of Computing Studies under the supervision of Professor He Wang. Before that, I received my B.E. degree from Wuhan University in 2019, supervised by Professor Qin Zou.

My current research focuses on 3D vision and computer graphics, with a particular interest in neural implicit representations for the reconstruction of visual, geometric, and physical properties of the dynamic world. I aim to apply these techniques to create immersive experiences in virtual and augmented reality, as well as in robotics applications.

I am seeking a Ph.D. position in the field of neural reconstruction and rendering, ideally starting in either 2023 or 2024.
